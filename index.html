Study Title: Urdu Generated Speech Accent Evaluation Study - TASK 2

Research Purpose: This study evaluates the accent nativeness of Urdu synthesized speech. Your evaluation will help us improve text-to-speech systems for the Urdu language.

What You Will Do:

  • Listen to 12 sentences with 6 comparison steps each
  • Steps 1-2: Compare Audio A to Audio B and rate how much Audio B improves the native accent of the speech. Mark errors in Audio A, and identify which words improved from A to B
  • Steps 3-6: Compare Audio A to Audio B on an accent nativeness scale. Identify which words are pronounced better or worse, and mark any errors in Audio B
  • Rate the accent naturalness on a scale for each comparison
  • Estimated time: 60-90 minutes

Confidentiality: Your responses will be anonymous and used only for research purposes.

Instructions & Reference Materials

How to Evaluate Each Sentence

For each sentence, you will complete 6 steps. At each step:

  1. Listen to Audio A and Audio B
  2. For Steps 1-2: Identify words in Audio B that are improved compared to Audio A
  3. For Steps 3-6: Mark any errors in Audio B if they exist
  4. Rate the accent naturalness on the provided scale

Error Codes & Accent Indicators

Code Error Type Description Examples
[P] PHONEME Error Segmental phonetic error - incorrect individual sound pronunciation Retroflex as alveolar; Wrong aspiration
[W] WORD/PROSODY Error Suprasegmental error - affects word-level properties Wrong stress; Unnatural pitch; Clitic separation
[S] SCRIPT-INDUCED Error Error from Urdu vs Perso-Arabic script confusion Emphatic vowels; Pharyngealization; Arabic patterns

📋 Table A: Urdu-Arabic Script Differences ▼

Common confusions between Urdu and Perso-Arabic script representation:

Symbol Arabic Urdu Error You Hear
ع Pharyngeal [ʕ] Velar [ɢ] Pharyngeal instead of soft velar
ح Pharyngeal [ħ] Velar [x] Harsh pharyngeal instead of soft
ق Emphatic [qˤ] Plain [q] Emphatic instead of plain
ض/ص/ط Emphatic Non-emphatic Emphasis spreading to vowels

📋 Table B: Accent Rating Scale ▼

Use this scale to rate the accent/naturalness in all 6 steps:

Rating Accent Level Description
1 Equally Foreign Both audios have equally strong foreign accent
2 Less Foreign Audio B sounds less foreign than Audio A
3 Neutral Audio B is neither native nor foreign
4 Almost Native Audio B sounds close to native Urdu speech
5 Native Audio B sounds like native Urdu speech
